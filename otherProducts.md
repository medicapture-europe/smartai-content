# **MediCapture Other Products: Product Specifications & Documentation**

## **MediCapture MTS Series Monitors: Product Specifications**

This section describes the specifications and operation parameters for MediCapture MTS series medical-grade touch monitors, including models **MTS101**, **MTS156**, and **MTS156W**. *(Note: Model MTS245W is currently under consideration, and its details are not yet provided. MTS245W could be available by the special order)*

### **General Device Description & Intended Use**

* **Device Description:** Monitor with integrated capacitive touchscreen.  
* **Device Capabilities:** Displays video or graphical content from an external host device; supports user interaction via tap, swipe, and pinch-to-zoom gestures.  
* **Intended Use:** Serves as a human-machine interface (HMI) for various medical devices.  
* **Device Grade:** Medical grade.  
* **Medical Device Classification:** Class 1\.  
* **Quality Management Standard:** ISO 13485\.

### **Design & Architecture**

* **Case Material:** High-durability aluminum alloy.  
* **Touchscreen Interface:** Capacitive touchscreen supporting up to **10 multi-touch points**.  
* **Touch Interface Protocol:** USB, standard HID device profile.  
* **Ingress Protection Level:** IP54 (front-panel face) and IP20 (rear enclosure case).  
* **Cooling Design:** Fanless construction (minimizes noise, prevents dust circulation, and limits pathogen distribution in sterile fields).  
* **Mounting Standards:** Back panel features standard mounting screw holes for 4 x M4x10 mm mounting screws (screws not included).  
* **Electrical Protection:** SELV (Safety Extra Low Voltage) class; IP-rated insulation.  
* **Data Privacy & Security:** Not applicable; MTS devices do not feature storage units and do not store user or patient data.

### **Compliance & Standard Certifications**

* **Medical Equipment Safety:** \* EN 60601-1:2006 \+ A1:2013 \+ A12:2014  
  * MDR 2017/745 EU  
* **Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC):** \* EN 60601-1-2:2015  
  * FCC Part 15B Class B  
* **Environmental & Hazard Protections:**  
  * EN IEC 63000:2018  
  * WEEE Directive  
* **Other Certifications:** \* IEC 61140:2016  
  * Low Voltage Directive (2014/35/EU)  
  * UKCA

### **Hardware & Software Compatibility**

* **MediCapture Recorders:** Seamlessly compatible with MVR-series medical video recorders.  
* **Third-Party Devices:** Compatible with any external host outputting video via HDMI or DVI alongside a USB port for touch controls.

### **General Installation & Operating Instructions**

* **Installation:** Mount securely onto a VESA 75 or VESA 100 compatible monitor arm or wall mount bracket. Interconnect the host device using appropriate HDMI and USB touch-control cables, and secure loose cables to prevent physical strain.  
* **Operating Principle:** Fully automatic Plug & Play.  
* **How to Turn ON:** Switch on the host system. The MTS monitor boots automatically once both active power and a viable video signal are present.  
* **How to Turn OFF:** Turn off the host system. *(Alternative method for MTS156: Press and hold the physical MENU key for 3 seconds.)*  
* **HDMI Port Function:** Serves as the primary video signal input.

## **MVC Pro SDI to HDMI Video Converter: Product Specifications**

### **Device Information and Core Specifications**

* **Device Name:** MVC Pro SDI to HDMI  
* **Device Category:** Medical Video Converter (MVC)  
* **Intended Use:** Downscales and converts high-end SDI feed outputs (e.g., from modern surgical cameras) to standard HDMI interfaces for viewing, routing, and recording.  
* **Device Grade:** Medical Grade (Class 1\)  
* **Chassis Material:** Rugged Stainless Steel  
* **Form Factor:** Table-top mountable

### **Functional Summary**

Designed to aggregate video streams from 4K Ultra HD (UHD) endoscopic/surgical cameras configured with quad 3G-SDI connectors. It integrates and transforms the signal into an HDMI stream delivered to an MVR-series recording device.

### **Device Variants & Ordering Information**

* **MVC410 (Quad Format):** Supports **Square Division (Quad Split)** input modes.  
* **MVC4102SI (2SI Format):** Supports **2-Sample-Interleave (2SI)** input modes.

### **Installation & Operating Routine**

1. **Physical Setup:** Place the MVC unit on an even surface near the surgical camera console.  
2. **SDI Connection:** Run up to 4 BNC cables from the surgical camera's SDI output interfaces to the input ports (SDI A/B/C/D) on the back of the MVC.  
3. **HDMI Connection:** Run HDMI lines from the MVC's HDMI outputs to your target medical monitor or MVR recorder. Secure all cables safely.  
4. **Boot Sequence:** Turn on the surgical camera. Switch the MVC's rear power switch to ON (indicated by **I**). Turn on the downstream monitor or recorder to view the processed stream.

### **Troubleshooting the MVC**

#### **Issue 1: A 4K camera is connected via four BNC cables, but the output displays as four identical 2x2 grid images.**

* **Possible Cause:** Mismatch between the surgical camera's physical video split output configuration and the MVC's firmware parameters (Square Division/Quad Split vs. 2SI/2-Sample-Interleave).  
* **Solutions:**  
  1. Access the camera's system menu or adjust physical DIP switches to change the SDI output format to align with your converter.  
  2. If the surgical camera's output format is locked, replace the converter unit with the matching alternate model (MVC410 for Quad/Square Division, or MVC4102SI for 2SI).

#### **Issue 2: A horizontal or vertical line cuts through the center of the video output display after some operational runtime.**

* **Possible Cause:** Temporary frame synchronization drift between the four independent input BNC cable lines.  
* **Solutions:**  
  1. Perform a manual hardware reset by toggling the MVC rear power switch OFF (**O**) and back ON (**I**).  
  2. If the sync loss happens frequently, inspect all BNC connections and replace any damaged, worn, or sub-par cables.  
  3. Ensure all four BNC interface cables are identical in standard, specification, and physical length to avoid phase and transmission latency discrepancies.
